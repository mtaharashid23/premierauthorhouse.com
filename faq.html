Main FAQ

The web is filled with digital writing and refining outlets, making it tough to filter out scams and identify a credible and honest partner. Here are several factors to verify when you are assessing agencies to ensure you choose a dependable firm for your projects:

  • 1.  Is the cost appropriate? If a deal feels too good to be true, it likely is. Crafting a high-end book takes immense effort and deep skill—do not be misled by low-grade writers offering "budget" rates.
  • 2.  What is the payment structure? You should fund ghostwriting in segments and never 100% at the start. Following the initial fee, you should pay only if you are satisfied with the draft. Ensure milestones are fixed so everyone understands the goals.
  • 3.  Who is the decision-maker? Ghostwriting is a team effort, but you must have the final word on all editorial paths. If a partner ignores your vision, it becomes their book instead of yours.
  • 4.  Who owns the legal rights? It is your project, meaning you must retain all intellectual rights. Avoid writers who demand a share of your ownership.
  • 5.  Is the project private? This is YOUR work. A writer’s name should not be included on the final draft unless you specifically request it. Complete discretion is a standard requirement for our service.

Regardless of the provider you select, ensure your work is protected. Always partner with a verified agency boasting proven professional history.

Finding a trustworthy partner online requires caution. Here are the essential questions to ask to ensure you choose a professional and reliable agency for your book:

  • 1.  Is the rate realistic? Quality work takes hundreds of hours. Do not trust ultra-low prices that compromise the integrity of your project.
  • 2.  Who owns the rights? It is your book; you should hold 100% of the rights. Be cautious of any service asking for a share in your royalties.
  • 3.  What is the payment plan? Always pay in segments based on progress. You should only fund the next stage if you are satisfied with the current draft.
  • 4.  Who controls the content? You must have the final word. A professional writer will prioritize your vision over their own preferences.
  • 5.   Freelancer vs Firm: Beware of lone freelancers who may be overbooked. An agency like ours offers continuity and reliability, providing replacements if needed without extra cost to you.
  • 6.  History: Ensure the firm has experience with published works. A top agency will have a robust staff of experts and connections to the publishing world.
